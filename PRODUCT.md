# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Procurement managers, engineering directors, and project leads at aerospace and automotive OEMs and Tier 1 suppliers. They arrive with a specific capability gap or vendor vetting need — they want to quickly establish whether Indval has the technical depth and organisational maturity to be trusted with high-stakes manufacturing and engineering work.

## Product Purpose

Indval Technologies' marketing site. Its job is to establish credibility, communicate technical capability across a broad service range (CNC, composites, MRO, BIW, etc.), and convert qualified visitors into enquiries. Success is a potential client who leaves confident enough to reach out.

## Brand Personality

Partner-first, technically credible, human. Indval is a collaborative firm — rigorous without being cold, expert without being inaccessible. The tone is confident but not arrogant: a firm that earns trust through precision and genuine engagement, not prestige signalling.

## Anti-references

- Generic SME contractor sites (GoDaddy-template look, clip-art icons, thin typography)
- Over-designed tech startups (gradient blobs, Framer-template aesthetics, "move fast" energy)
- AI-default outputs: eyebrows on every section, identical card grids, cream/sand body backgrounds, hero-metric templates, gradient text

## Design Principles

1. **Earn trust, don't claim it.** Specificity over superlatives. Real capability language, real people, real detail.
2. **Partner energy, not vendor energy.** Warm, accessible layouts and copy. Approachable without sacrificing technical authority.
3. **Precision in the details.** Spacing, alignment, and typographic hierarchy should feel engineered — not accidental.
4. **No visual noise.** Every element earns its place. Restraint over decoration.
5. **Human faces and names are assets.** Leadership, team, and contact moments should feel personal, not corporate.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Engineering clients include senior stakeholders who may use assistive tools; text contrast must be rock-solid. Reduced-motion support required for all transitions.
